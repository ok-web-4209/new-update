Local Defense in Hollywood

A criminal charge in Hollywood can put your job, your record, your license, and your family under real pressure, and the system is built so that power skews toward the state. Hoffman Legal exists to level that fight — with honest advice and a defense built around the facts of your case.

Hollywood operates its own police department rather than contracting with the county, so Hollywood officers handle the stops, DUI investigations, and arrests you may be facing — from the beach Broadwalk to the SR-7 corridor.

Where Hollywood Cases Are Heard

Hollywood is home to the Broward South Regional Courthouse at 3550 Hollywood Boulevard, where misdemeanor, traffic, and county cases from the south end of the county are heard. Felony matters move to the Central Courthouse in Fort Lauderdale, and both sit within the 17th Judicial Circuit.

After a Hollywood arrest, most people are booked into the Broward Sheriff's Office Main Jail at 555 SE 1st Avenue in Fort Lauderdale and are brought before a judge for a first appearance within 24 hours, where bond and conditions of release are set. Having a lawyer involved at that early stage can make a real difference.

Enforcement & Crash Corridors

Hollywood's crashes and enforcement stops cluster on a few well-known roads, several of them kept busy around the clock by the casino and beach traffic:

  • State Road 7 (US-441) at Sheridan Street — one of the city's highest-crash intersections, kept busy 24/7 by the nearby Seminole Hard Rock Hotel & Casino.
  • State Road 7 and Hollywood Boulevard — heavy transit and commercial traffic and a persistent crash hotspot.
  • US-1 (Federal Highway) and Sheridan Street — busy corridors with frequent rear-end and turning collisions.
  • Hollywood Beach and the Broadwalk area — pedestrians, cyclists, and nightlife where DUI and disorderly-conduct allegations are common on weekends.

What to Do After an Arrest in Hollywood

  • Stay silent about the incident. Be polite, but do not explain or argue. You have the right to remain silent — use it.
  • Do not consent to searches of your car, phone, or home until you have spoken with a lawyer.
  • Write down what you remember while it is fresh: the location, the stated reason for the stop, what was said, and any witnesses.
  • Call a lawyer before your first appearance, which in Broward usually happens within 24 hours and sets bond and release conditions.
  • Do not discuss the case on recorded jail calls, with friends, or on social media.

Hollywood Criminal Defense FAQ

Which courthouse handles Hollywood criminal cases?

Misdemeanor and traffic cases from Hollywood are generally heard at the Broward South Regional Courthouse at 3550 Hollywood Boulevard, while felonies go to the Central Courthouse in Fort Lauderdale. Both are part of the 17th Judicial Circuit.

Who arrests people in Hollywood?

Hollywood has its own police department, so Hollywood officers handle most stops and arrests in the city. After arrest, people are typically booked into the BSO Main Jail in Fort Lauderdale, and the Broward State Attorney prosecutes.

What areas of Hollywood see the most arrests?

Enforcement is heavy along State Road 7 near the Seminole Hard Rock, on Hollywood Boulevard, on US-1, and around the beach and Broadwalk, especially on nights and weekends.
