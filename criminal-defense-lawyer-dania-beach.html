Where Dania Beach Cases Are Heard

Dania Beach falls within Florida's 17th Judicial Circuit, which covers all of Broward County. Which courthouse your case goes to depends on the charge:

Misdemeanor & Traffic

Broward South Regional Courthouse, 3550 Hollywood Blvd, Hollywood, FL 33021 — the closest court to Dania Beach for county-level and traffic matters.

Felony Cases

Broward County Central Courthouse, 201 SE 6th Street, Fort Lauderdale, FL 33301 — where felony charges and first appearances are handled.

Booking & Jail

Broward Sheriff's Office Main Jail, 555 SE 1st Avenue, Fort Lauderdale, FL 33301, is where most people are booked after a Dania Beach arrest.

Prosecution

State charges are brought by the Broward State Attorney's Office. Knowing how that office charges and negotiates cases matters to your outcome.

Policing & Enforcement in Dania Beach

Dania Beach contracts its policing to the Broward Sheriff's Office (BSO) Dania Beach District rather than operating a separate city police department. In practice, that means BSO deputies make the traffic stops, DUI investigations, and arrests you may be dealing with.

Enforcement tends to concentrate along the city's busiest corridors and destinations, including:

  • I-95, US-1 (Federal Highway), and State Road A1A — heavy traffic and frequent stops, especially near the airport interchange.
  • Griffin Road and Sheridan Street — primary east-west commuter routes and airport access roads.
  • Dania Pointe, The Casino at Dania Beach, and beachside venues — areas where DUI, disorderly conduct, and drug allegations commonly arise on nights and weekends.
  • Port Everglades and airport perimeter — a zone with added state and federal law-enforcement presence.

The details of a stop — whether there was lawful reason to pull you over, how field sobriety exercises were conducted, whether a search was consented to or supported by probable cause — are often where a Dania Beach case is won or lost. We review that record closely.

What a Conviction Can Cost You

The formal penalty is only part of the picture. Beyond any jail time, fines, or probation, a Florida criminal conviction can follow you for years — affecting employment and professional licenses, housing applications, a security clearance, immigration status, and your ability to be around firearms. Even a withhold of adjudication or a plea that seems convenient in the moment can carry consequences that are hard to undo later. Understanding the full weight of what is at stake, before you make any decision, is exactly why an early consultation matters.

What to Do After an Arrest in Dania Beach

  • Stay silent about the incident. Be polite to deputies, but do not explain, argue, or confess. You have the right to remain silent — use it.
  • Do not consent to searches of your car, phone, or home without speaking to a lawyer first.
  • Write down everything you remember while it is fresh: the location, the reason given for the stop, what was said, and any witnesses.
  • Call a lawyer before your first appearance. In Broward, that hearing usually happens within 24 hours, and early involvement can affect bond and release conditions.
  • Do not discuss the case on recorded jail calls, with friends, or on social media.

Dania Beach Criminal Defense FAQ

Which courthouse will handle my Dania Beach criminal case?

It depends on the charge. Misdemeanor, traffic, and county cases from Dania Beach are generally heard at the Broward South Regional Courthouse at 3550 Hollywood Boulevard in Hollywood, while felonies go to the Broward County Central Courthouse at 201 SE 6th Street in Fort Lauderdale. Both are part of the 17th Judicial Circuit, and Hoffman Legal appears in both.

Who arrests people in Dania Beach?

Dania Beach has no municipal police department; policing is handled by the Broward Sheriff's Office Dania Beach District. After an arrest, most people are booked into the BSO Main Jail at 555 SE 1st Avenue in Fort Lauderdale, and charges are prosecuted by the Broward State Attorney's Office.

Should I talk to the police before calling a lawyer?

No. You have the right to remain silent and the right to an attorney. Politely decline to answer questions about the incident and do not consent to searches until you have spoken with a lawyer. Call Hoffman Legal at (954) 459-4236 first.

How soon will I see a judge after a Dania Beach arrest?

In Broward County, most people are brought before a magistrate for a first appearance within 24 hours, where bond and conditions of release are addressed. Having a lawyer involved early can make a meaningful difference.
