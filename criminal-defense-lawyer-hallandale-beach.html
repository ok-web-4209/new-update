Where Hallandale Beach Cases Are Heard

Hallandale Beach sits at the southern edge of Broward County. Misdemeanor and traffic cases are generally heard at the Broward South Regional Courthouse at 3550 Hollywood Boulevard, just to the north, while felonies go to the Central Courthouse in Fort Lauderdale — all within Florida's 17th Judicial Circuit.

After a Hallandale Beach arrest, most people are booked into the Broward Sheriff's Office Main Jail at 555 SE 1st Avenue in Fort Lauderdale and are brought before a judge for a first appearance within 24 hours, where bond and conditions of release are set. What to Do After an Arrest in Hallandale Beach

  • Stay silent about the incident. Be polite, but do not explain or argue. You have the right to remain silent — use it.
  • Do not consent to searches of your car, phone, or home until you have spoken with a lawyer.
  • Write down what you remember while it is fresh: the location, the stated reason for the stop, what was said, and any witnesses.
  • Call a lawyer before your first appearance, which in Broward usually happens within 24 hours and sets bond and release conditions.
  • Do not discuss the case on recorded jail calls, with friends, or on social media.
