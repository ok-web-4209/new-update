Arrested in Hallandale Beach: Where Your Case Goes

The Hallandale Beach Police Department handles most arrests inside the city, from the Hallandale Beach Boulevard and US-1 corridors to the beach and the Gulfstream Park area. The city has no courthouse of its own, so cases route north: misdemeanor and county matters generally to the Broward South Regional Courthouse, 3550 Hollywood Boulevard in neighboring Hollywood, and felonies to the Broward County Central Courthouse in Fort Lauderdale, where booking at the BSO Main Jail and first appearance — generally within 24 hours — also occur. All of it sits in the 17th Judicial Circuit under the Broward State Attorney's Office.

Misdemeanors & Traffic

The Cases a Border City Produces

Hallandale Beach packs beach traffic, nightlife, high-rise living, and a county line into a few square miles — and its docket reflects that:

  • DUI and traffic-stop cases along US-1, Hallandale Beach Boulevard, and I-95, including stops just over the Miami-Dade line that raise jurisdiction questions.
  • Battery and disorderly conduct arising from nightlife, the beach, and the Gulfstream Park entertainment area.
  • Domestic-violence allegations and injunctions from the city's dense residential towers — cases where an accusation alone can cost you your home and reputation before any evidence is tested.
  • Drug possession charges from vehicle searches and street encounters.
  • Theft and fraud allegations from the retail corridors.

Many Hallandale Beach defendants are seasonal residents or visitors, which adds a practical layer: court obligations in a county you do not live in. In many matters, local counsel can appear on your behalf at routine hearings and manage the case while you live your life — ask about your specific charge.

Protect Yourself From the First Minute

  • Invoke your rights, politely. "I am not answering questions and I want a lawyer" ends the interview without making anything worse.
  • Never consent to a search of your car, phone, home, or condo — make the State justify it later.
  • Assume every jail call is recorded, because it is. Discuss the case only with your attorney.
  • Move fast on counsel. First appearance generally happens within 24 hours, and the days before the State files formal charges are a window where defense input can change what gets filed.
  • Think past the sentence. Immigration status, professional licenses, and future record sealing can all turn on how the case resolves — not just whether you "get probation."

Hallandale Beach Criminal Defense FAQ

Where will my Hallandale Beach case be heard?

The city has no courthouse, so misdemeanor and county-level cases generally go to the Broward South Regional Courthouse at 3550 Hollywood Boulevard, and felonies to the Broward County Central Courthouse in Fort Lauderdale. Both are 17th Judicial Circuit courts where Hoffman Legal appears regularly.

I was stopped near the county line. Does Broward or Miami-Dade handle my case?

Generally, the county where the offense allegedly occurred prosecutes it — so a stop on the Hallandale Beach side goes to Broward courts, while conduct over the line goes to Miami-Dade. Border-area cases sometimes raise genuine venue and jurisdiction questions worth examining. Hoffman Legal serves clients throughout both counties.

I'm a seasonal resident. Do I have to stay in Florida for my case?

Often not for every hearing. In many misdemeanor matters, and at various stages of other cases, your attorney can appear on your behalf; travel and appearance requirements depend on the charge and the judge. Tell your lawyer your situation up front so court obligations and your life elsewhere can be managed together.

Someone filed for a restraining order against me in Hallandale Beach. Is that criminal?

An injunction petition is civil, but it moves fast, can remove you from your home, restrict firearms, and create a record — and violating it is a crime. Injunction hearings often run parallel to criminal charges from the same incident, so coordinated defense matters. See our injunctions page, and do not attend the hearing unrepresented.
