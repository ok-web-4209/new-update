First Steps After a Hollywood Crash

Whatever the crash looked like, the same early moves protect you: call 911 and get the collision documented, accept or seek medical evaluation quickly, photograph the vehicles and the scene, and collect the other driver's license, tag, and insurance details along with witness contact information. If the other car was a rental or rideshare vehicle, photograph any rental-company sticker or trade dress — that detail changes which insurance policies apply. And before you speak with any insurance company on a recorded line, talk to an attorney about what you are required to share and what you are not.

Why Hollywood Traffic Is Its Own Animal

Hollywood stretches from the beach to the western suburbs, and its crash patterns change block by block. Hollywood Boulevard funnels commuters and visitors through the heart of the city and its downtown. Sheridan Street carries fast east–west traffic across nearly the full width of the city. State Road 7/US-441 is a dense commercial corridor of shopping plazas and driveways where cars constantly enter and exit traffic. And along A1A, beach traffic mixes drivers looking for parking with pedestrians, cyclists, and out-of-town visitors unfamiliar with the area.

These differences matter legally. A driveway pullout collision on the SR-7 corridor raises different fault questions than a lane-change crash on Sheridan Street or a low-speed impact in beach traffic on A1A. An attorney who actually knows these roads can evaluate your version of events against the physical reality of where it happened — and explain it persuasively to an adjuster or a jury.

Tourists, Rental Cars, and Out-of-State Drivers

A meaningful share of collisions here involve a visitor: a tourist in a rental car, a snowbird with an out-of-state policy, or a rideshare driver working the beach and airport corridors. Each situation adds a layer to the insurance picture:

  • Rental cars may involve the renter's personal policy, coverage purchased at the rental counter, and the rental company's own coverage — in an order that depends on the contract and the policies.
  • Out-of-state drivers carry policies written under other states' rules, which can affect available coverage and how the claim is handled.
  • Rideshare vehicles are covered differently depending on whether the app was off, waiting for a ride, or carrying a passenger. Our rideshare accidents page covers those tiers in detail.

Identifying every policy that applies — and putting each carrier on notice promptly — is one of the most valuable things a lawyer does in these cases. It is much harder to do after the visitor has flown home.

Injuries and Medical Documentation

Crash injuries here run the same painful range we see everywhere: whiplash, concussions, shoulder and knee damage, fractures, and back injuries that surface days later. Many seriously injured crash victims in this area are treated at Memorial Regional Hospital at 3501 Johnson Street in Hollywood. Wherever you receive care, your medical records do double duty — they guide your recovery and they document your claim. Keep discharge paperwork, follow every referral, and do not skip appointments; gaps in treatment are one of the first things insurers point to when they argue an injury is minor.

Florida PIP and the 14-Day Rule

Florida's no-fault system means your own Personal Injury Protection (PIP) coverage is generally the first source of payment for medical bills and lost wages after a crash, regardless of who caused it, up to the policy limit (commonly $10,000). To be eligible for PIP medical benefits, you generally must get initial treatment within 14 days of the collision.

Two things people often get wrong about that rule: first, it applies to PIP medical benefits — missing the window can forfeit those benefits, but it does not automatically wipe out every other claim you might have, which depends on your circumstances. Second, PIP is rarely the whole story. If your injuries meet Florida's threshold for serious injuries, you may pursue the at-fault driver for damages that PIP never covers, including pain and suffering. A consultation sorts out which paths are open in your specific case.

What Your Claim May Be Worth

No honest lawyer quotes a settlement number on a webpage — the value of a claim depends on your injuries, your treatment, the available coverage, and how clearly fault can be shown. What we can say is which categories of damages Florida law recognizes: medical expenses (past and future), lost income and earning capacity, property damage, and, where the threshold is met, pain and suffering and loss of enjoyment of life. Our job is to document each category thoroughly so nothing is quietly left out of the negotiation.

Understanding Contingency Fees

Most personal injury cases are handled on a contingency fee — you pay nothing up front, and the attorney's fee is a percentage of the recovery only if you win. If there's no recovery, you owe no attorney's fee.

Before we take your case, the written fee agreement spells out the exact percentage and how case costs are handled, so there are no surprises later.

Hollywood Car Accident FAQ

I was hit by a tourist in a rental car in Hollywood. Who pays?

Potentially several sources: your own PIP first, then some combination of the renter's personal auto policy, coverage purchased from the rental company, and the rental company's coverage — the order depends on the contracts and policies involved. Identifying and notifying each carrier quickly matters, especially before the visitor leaves Florida.

What if my crash happened near Hollywood Beach or on A1A?

Beach-area crashes often involve low speeds but real injuries, plus complications like pedestrians, cyclists, and drivers unfamiliar with the area. Photograph the scene and get witness names before the crowd disperses. Even low-speed impacts deserve medical evaluation — and the 14-day PIP window applies no matter how minor the crash seemed.

The other driver lives out of state. Does that hurt my claim?

Not necessarily. Out-of-state drivers are subject to Florida law for crashes that happen here, and their insurers handle Florida claims routinely. The differences show up in the policy details — coverage types and limits vary by state — which is why we obtain and read the actual policy rather than assume.

I was treated at Memorial Regional Hospital. What records do I need?

Keep everything: emergency department records, imaging reports, discharge instructions, referrals, and every bill. Your medical records are the backbone of the claim — they connect your injuries to the crash and document the cost of your care. We can help gather complete certified records once we are involved.

Where would a Hollywood accident claim be filed?

Most claims resolve with insurers before any lawsuit. If suit becomes necessary, cases arising in Hollywood are generally filed in Broward County's courts; some matters are heard at the South Regional Courthouse at 3550 Hollywood Boulevard, while others proceed in Fort Lauderdale. The right venue depends on your case, and we handle that analysis.
