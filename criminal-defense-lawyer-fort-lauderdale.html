Unlike some neighboring cities, Fort Lauderdale runs its own municipal police department, and its officers make most of the traffic stops and arrests downtown, along the beach, and on the highways that cut through the city.

Where Fort Lauderdale Cases Are Heard

Fort Lauderdale is the seat of Broward County, so the Broward County Central Courthouse at 201 SE 6th Street — where felony cases and first appearances are handled — is right downtown. County and traffic matters are heard in the same 17th Judicial Circuit, and the Broward State Attorney's Office, headquartered here, prosecutes the charges.

After a Fort Lauderdale arrest, most people are booked into the Broward Sheriff's Office Main Jail at 555 SE 1st Avenue in Fort Lauderdale and are brought before a judge for a first appearance within 24 hours, where bond and conditions of release are set. Having a lawyer involved at that early stage can make a real difference.

Enforcement & Crash Corridors

As the busiest city in Broward, Fort Lauderdale concentrates enforcement and collisions along a handful of heavily traveled corridors:

  • I-95 — including the stretch known as one of the deadliest miles in the state, between the I-595 interchange and Marina Mile Boulevard.
  • US-1 (Federal Highway) — dense with intersections and pedestrians, especially between Sunrise Boulevard and SE 17th Street.
  • Broward Boulevard, Sunrise Boulevard, and Oakland Park Boulevard — high-volume east-west arteries with frequent signalized crashes.
  • Las Olas Boulevard and the beach district — heavy foot traffic, nightlife, and tourists, where pedestrian, bicycle, and DUI incidents cluster.

What to Do After an Arrest in Fort Lauderdale

  • Stay silent about the incident. Be polite, but do not explain or argue. You have the right to remain silent — use it.
  • Do not consent to searches of your car, phone, or home until you have spoken with a lawyer.
  • Write down what you remember while it is fresh: the location, the stated reason for the stop, what was said, and any witnesses.
  • Call a lawyer before your first appearance, which in Broward usually happens within 24 hours and sets bond and release conditions.
  • Do not discuss the case on recorded jail calls, with friends, or on social media.

Fort Lauderdale Criminal Defense FAQ

Where will my Fort Lauderdale criminal case be heard?

Felony cases and first appearances are handled at the Broward County Central Courthouse at 201 SE 6th Street in downtown Fort Lauderdale. Misdemeanor and traffic matters are heard in the same 17th Judicial Circuit, and the Broward State Attorney's Office prosecutes the charges.

Who makes arrests in Fort Lauderdale?

Fort Lauderdale has its own municipal police department. Its officers make most stops and arrests within the city, though the Broward Sheriff's Office and state agencies also operate in the area. After arrest, most people are booked into the BSO Main Jail on SE 1st Avenue.

Should I talk to detectives before hiring a lawyer?

No. Politely decline to answer questions about the incident and ask for a lawyer. Statements to Fort Lauderdale police can be used against you, and you have the right to remain silent. Call Hoffman Legal at (954) 459-4236 first.

Can a first-time charge be kept off my record?

Sometimes. Depending on the charge and your history, options like a diversion program or a withhold of adjudication may help you avoid a conviction, and some records can later be sealed or expunged. An early consultation is the best way to learn what applies.
