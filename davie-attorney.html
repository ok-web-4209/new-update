Arrested in Davie: What You're Actually Facing

The Davie Police Department makes most arrests in town, with county and state agencies on I-595 and the turnpike approaches. Cases flow into the 17th Judicial Circuit: felonies and first appearances at the Broward County Central Courthouse in Fort Lauderdale (booking at the BSO Main Jail, magistrate within about 24 hours), and county-level matters assigned among the circuit's courthouses, including the West Regional Courthouse serving central-west Broward.

Davie's docket skews young: DUI and alcohol-related arrests, drug possession from traffic stops and dorm-adjacent encounters, disorderly conduct, and retail theft. For a student, the real sentence is rarely the fine — it is the university's conduct process, the internship background check, the professional-school application. That long view drives how we defend these cases: diversion programs and withholds where they protect the record, motions where the stop or search fails scrutiny, and a clear map to sealing or expunging whatever can later be cleared. Our guide to withholds of adjudication explains the outcome many first cases should be aiming at.

Injured in Davie: Roads, Campuses, and Horses

University Drive and Davie Road carry dense student and commuter traffic past a long row of signalized intersections; State Road 84 and the I-595 interchanges add high-speed merges. The result is the full crash menu — rear-end chains, left-turn collisions, and serious motorcycle and pedestrian impacts around campus areas. Florida's no-fault rules put your own PIP first, with its 14-day treatment window, and claims against at-fault drivers or UM coverage carrying the larger losses.

Davie's rural-estate character adds cases most suburbs never see — incidents involving horses and trails, plus the usual premises and dog-bite claims from apartments and shopping plazas. Whatever the setting, the playbook holds: prompt medical care, preserved evidence, and no recorded statements to insurers before advice.

The First 72 Hours in a Davie Case

Arrested: the sequence is booking, first appearance within about a day, then the State's filing decision — and each stage rewards early counsel. Invoke your rights politely, consent to nothing, stay off social media, and write out the whole night while it is fresh: the stop location on University Drive or SR-84, the reason given, every instruction. Students should loop in counsel before answering any university inquiry, because campus and criminal processes feed each other.

Injured: treatment within the 14-day PIP window comes first, then the evidence sweep — photos, witnesses, crash report, and quick preservation requests for camera footage along the campus corridors. Ramp and merge crashes near I-595 are fault fights from day one; what you capture in week one is usually what wins them.

Davie FAQ

Where do Davie criminal cases get heard?

Felonies and first appearances proceed at the Broward County Central Courthouse in Fort Lauderdale; county-level and misdemeanor matters are assigned among the 17th Judicial Circuit's courthouses, including the West Regional Courthouse serving central-west Broward. Assignment depends on the charge, and we confirm it immediately once a case exists.

My student was arrested in Davie. Will the university find out?

Possibly — arrests are public records, and universities have conduct processes that can run parallel to court. That is a reason to act quickly, not panic: how the criminal case resolves often shapes the school outcome, and resolutions that avoid conviction protect both. Discuss the specific school's process at the consultation.

I was rear-ended on University Drive. Whose insurance pays?

Your own PIP generally pays first for initial medical bills regardless of fault — start treatment within the 14-day window — while a claim against the at-fault driver may cover what PIP does not, including pain and suffering for qualifying injuries. If the driver is uninsured, UM coverage on your policy may respond. A free review maps the layers.

Can a first arrest in Davie stay off my record permanently?

Sometimes, depending on the charge and outcome. Diversion programs may end in dismissal (pointing toward expungement), and withholds of adjudication may preserve sealing eligibility. Florida generally allows one lifetime seal or expunge, so the strategy is chosen carefully. No outcome can be promised — but the goal is always on the table from day one.

Do I need a lawyer based in Davie?
